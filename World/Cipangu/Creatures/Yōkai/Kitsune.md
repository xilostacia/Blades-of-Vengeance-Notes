**Classification:** Yōkai  
**Nature:** Fox-born spirit  
**Reputation:** Untrustworthy, ill omen  
**Status:** Rare, persecuted

## Overview

Kitsune are fox-born yōkai spirits who awaken to sentience after long years steeped in the land’s flowing [[Ki]]. They walk the boundary between beast and spirit, neither wholly animal nor fully mortal. Mischievous by nature and masters of shapeshifting, kitsune frequently take human form to conceal their vixen origins.

While shapechanging beings are not uncommon within Cipangu, kitsune are regarded as especially untrustworthy, even among other [[Yōkai]].

---
## Physical Traits
- True form resembles a fox spirit.
- Mature kitsune grow multiple tails.
- Each tail reflects accumulated arcane influence and spiritual power.
- Tails are highly valued and believed to possess healing properties.

Because of this, kitsune are frequently hunted.

---
## Cultural Reputation

Among common folk:
- Seen as omens of ill fortune.
- Associated with deception and manipulation.
- Feared more than revered.

Among samurai:
- Actively hunted.
- Viewed as dangerous infiltrators.
- Considered threats to social order.

Among yōkai:
- Regarded as clever but untrustworthy.
- Rarely fully accepted.

---
## Lifespan
Kitsune are said to be practically immortal.

However:
- Few survive long enough to confirm this.
- Hunting and persecution have drastically reduced their numbers.

---
## Current Status in Cipangu
Due to relentless persecution:
- Kitsune numbers have dwindled greatly.
- Most now remain hidden.
- Living openly is nearly impossible.

They survive by blending into human society or retreating into remote regions.
